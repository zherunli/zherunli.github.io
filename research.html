Research Projects

My research connects robotics, CAD/CAM, coordinate measurement, and deep learning, with an emphasis on efficient inspection and intelligent manufacturing.

Funded Research

National Natural Science Foundation of China Project

Core Contributor | Shanghai, China | 2023 – 2025

  • Conducted research on five-axis inspection path planning for complex curved surfaces.
  • Proposed a dual-NURBS-based five-axis path generation method to address path invalidation caused by fitting errors.
  • Enabled executable zero-deviation sweep scanning paths and improved both path generation efficiency and measurement accuracy.

Shanghai Natural Science Foundation Project

Core Contributor | Shanghai, China | 2023 – 2025

  • Developed a five-axis continuous scanning method for curvature variable surfaces.
  • Reduced slipping risk by actively controlling probe posture during measurement.
  • Improved measurement efficiency by approximately 80% compared with conventional methods.

NSFC Young Scientists Fund Project

Core Contributor | Shanghai, China | 2023 – 2025

  • Designed an optimal fixturing algorithm for complex aero-engine impeller inspection.

Research Themes

Five-Axis Sweep Scanning

Path generation and posture optimization for blade cross sections and curvature-variable surfaces.

NURBS-Based Path Smoothing

Dual-NURBS and smoothing strategies that maintain executable probe trajectories under geometric constraints.

Impeller Inspection Planning

Setup minimization, accessibility analysis, and adaptive inspection path planning for aero-engine impellers.

AI + CAD/CAM

Long-term interest in combining CAD/CAM knowledge, robotics, and learning-based methods for advanced manufacturing.
